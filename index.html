How it works

Three roles, one simple flow.

1. Villager

Sees upcoming Umuganda and confirms whether they will attend. If unavailable, they provide a reason.

2. Leader

Schedules activities and, on Umuganda day, cross-checks who confirmed and who came. Marks Present or Absent. Charges absentees.

3. Inspector

Views overall reports — activities, attendance rates, charges issued — to ensure community work runs smoothly.

What is Umuganda?

Umuganda is a Rwandan tradition of community work held on the last Saturday of every month. Citizens come together to clean, build and improve their neighbourhood — strengthening unity and shared responsibility.

This platform makes attendance fair and transparent: villagers confirm in advance, leaders verify on the day, and inspectors keep the whole system accountable.
